# COMMA: A microsimulation model to investigate the impact of covid-19 lockdown policies on depression rates 

(Word count: 249)

During the COVID-19 pandemic, lockdowns decreased COVID-19-related mortality and hospitalizations, but also took a large toll on populations' mental health, particularly on depression. We hypothesized that depression rates during the pandemic could be associated with the duration, sequence, and severity of lockdown policies, as evidenced by the growing literature on the effects of lockdown on mental health issues.

To test this, we developed COMMA (**CO**vid **M**ental-health **M**odel with **A**gents), a microsimulation model based on an individual-level synthetic population derived from data of the Groningen area's lifelines biobank ((n=2,556), [https://www.lifelines.nl/](https://www.lifelines.nl/)). This biobank provides rich socio-economic, demographic, activity, and health data, including depression onset and progression from pre- to post- pandemic periods. COMMA operates on a daily timestep, simulating a series of daily activities reflective of each individual's characteristics and current lockdown policies. The type of daily activities allowed in the model is configured to reflect actual lockdown policies applied in the Netherlands, but the model accepts also hypothetical/alternative lockdown scenarios. These activities, varying in accordance with actual or hypothetical lockdown conditions, were calibrated to influence depression rates positively or negatively, based on the literature surrounding lockdown-induced mental health issues, with the most profound impact noted among those quarantined due to COVID-19 infection.

COMMA not only aids in confirming existing literature on mental health impacts of lockdown measures but also offers a platform to explore potential alternative lockdown scenarios, to shed light into the factors that are the primary drivers of depression during COVID-19 lockdown, and thus potentially inform future policies in case of a future pandemic.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, lockdowns decreased COVID-19-related mortality and hospitalizations, but also took a large toll on populations’ mental health, particularly on depression. The diverse approaches to lockdowns —- varying in length, severity, and sequence, and continuously adjusted in response to the evolving understanding of the virus -— complicate the understanding of their direct impact on depression rates. It is also not clear if depression rates might rebound to pre-pandemic levels in the future.

To address these complexities, we propose to use a microsimulation model COMMA (COvid Mental-health Model with Agents) to elucidate the relationship between varying lockdown scenarios, including actual and hypothetical ones, and drepression rates. COMMA uses microsimulation methods to estimate the effects of lockdown policies on the onset and progression of depression across a synthetized agents population over time.

Operating on a daily timestep, COMMA simulates individual daily activities that are contingent on personal characteristics and lockdown conditions, including quarantine due to COVID-19 infection. These activities are designed to directly influence depression rates, drawing on prior literature on lockdown-induced mental health impacts, and leveraging data from the lifelines bio-bank, time-locked to a specific lockdown period.

We present the results from deploying COMMA to simulate the impacts of actual and hypothetical lockdown policies in the Netherlands on a synthetic population. The population data is derived from the Groningen area’s lifelines biobank ((n=2,556), [https://www.lifelines.nl/](https://www.lifelines.nl/)). This model provides the first attempt at clarifying the multifaceted relationship between lockdown policies and mental health outcomes, which is often overlooked when deciding lockdown policies aimed at curbing mortality and hospitalizations.
