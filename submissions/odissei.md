# COMMA: A microsimulation model of investigating impact of covid-19 lockdown policies on depression rates (Word count: 249)

During the COVID-19 pandemic, lockdowns decreased COVID-19-related mortality and hospitalizations, but also took a large toll on populations\'92 mental health, particularly on depression. We hypothesized that depression rates during the pandemic could be associated with the duration, sequence, and severity of lockdown policies, as evidenced by the growing literature on the effects of lockdown on mental health issues.\

To test this, we developed COMMA (**CO**vid **M**ental-health **M**odel with **A**gents), a microsimulation model based on an individual-level synthetic population derived from data of the Groningen area\'92s lifelines biobank ([https://www.lifelines.nl/](https://www.lifelines.nl/)). This biobank provides rich socio-economic, demographic, activity, and health data, including depression onset and progression from pre- to post- pandemic periods. COMMA operates on a daily timestep, simulating a series of daily activities reflective of each individual's characteristics and current lockdown policies. The type of daily activities allowed in the model is configured to reflect actual lockdown policies applied in the Netherlands, but the model accepts also hypothetical/alternative lockdown scenarios. These activities, varying in accordance with actual or hypothetical lockdown conditions, were calibrated to influence depression rates positively or negatively, based on the literature surrounding lockdown-induced mental health issues, with the most profound impact noted among those quarantined due to COVID-19 infection.\

COMMA not only aids in confirming existing literature on mental health impacts of lockdown measures but also offers a platform to explore potential alternative lockdown scenarios, to shed light into the factors that are the primary drivers of depression during COVID-19 lockdown, and thus potentially inform future policies in case of a future pandemic.}
